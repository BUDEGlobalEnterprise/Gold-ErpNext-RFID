# Consolidated Product Direction

## Combined Vision

"Zevar is the first native, cloud-deployable command-and-intelligence platform purpose-built for multi-store jewelry retail — a single Frappe/ERPNext core that fuses a real-time Live Monitor wall, a deep Sales Monitor, true jewelry Profit Intelligence, and a payroll-grade Workforce Intelligence suite into one role-aware product. Where The Edge owns jewelry data depth but is trapped on-prem with static desktop reports, where Lightspeed and Shopify own cloud UX but model COGS as a single flat number and have no native workforce/commission engine, and where RICS/Valigara/Jewel360 each nail one slice (RICS reporting grammar, Valigara metal-pricing, Jewel360 commissions) but none combine them — Zevar unifies all four. An owner opens one live wall and sees every store's sales, repairs, memos out on loan, gold-spot exposure, margin-at-risk, and associate scoreboard updating in real time over socket.io; drills from any tile into a margin waterfall that decomposes every dollar of COGS into gold-at-sale, gemstone, labor, making charge, payment interchange, and ABC overhead; simulates a gold-rate or discount change against projected volume before applying it; and pays associates on gross-profit-margin-tiered commissions that flow straight into salary slips. The Frappe stack (Frappe Insights for zero-code owner BI, HRMS Appraisal/Goal/Salary-Component for auditable workforce, realtime publish_realtime for the live wall, and a single MariaDB data model serving POS + HR + BI with no ETL) means Zevar delivers what rivals charge premium SaaS tiers for — at zero marginal software cost, self-hosted or cloud, infinitely customizable. The goal is not to match any one competitor but to be the refined superset: deeper on jewelry economics than The Edge, more live than Lightspeed, more workforce-native than Shopify, and more profitable-aware than any generic retail POS."

## Differentiation Thesis

"Zevar wins on a three-layer moat no single competitor can replicate. (1) JEWELRY ECONOMIC DEPTH: gold-rate-at-sale locking, per-karat purity mapping, gemstone 4Cs, making/alloy/labor decomposition, and payment-interchange-aware contribution margin — captured once per invoice in the Sale Cost Breakdown doctype and queried consistently everywhere. The Edge has this depth on-prem but as static desktop reports; Lightspeed, Shopify, and RICS model COGS as a single weighted-average number and cannot show true jewelry margin. (2) NATIVE UNIFICATION ON THE FRAPPE STACK: the live wall (socket.io realtime), the BI dashboards (Frappe Insights, zero backend code), the workforce suite (HRMS Appraisal Cycle + Goal tree + Salary Component for payroll-grade, audit-compliant commissions and quarterly reviews), and the profit engine all read ONE MariaDB data model — no ETL, no separate commission DB, no brittle sync. Competitors either bolt commission (Spiff/CaptivateIQ) or BI (Lightspeed Insights/Shopify Plus) onto the POS as paid external layers with latency and matching errors, or silo workforce in a separate SaaS (Deputy/Centrical). Zevar's commission engine is native to the doctype layer — a structural trust and cost advantage. (3) REAL-TIME + RELATIONSHIP: because Zevar owns the POS event stream, it can push live scoreboards, fire in-the-moment coaching nudges, and trigger gamification celebrations in real time — competitors relying on nightly CRM sync cannot. Layered on a relationship-driven clienteling model (book-of-business, bridal/timepiece appointments, follow-up accountability) rather than transaction-chasing leaderboards, Zevar becomes the only platform that is simultaneously cloud-native, jewelry-deep, workforce-native, and profit-intelligent — the refined superset the fragmented jewelry-POS market has been waiting for."

- Store-vs-store comparison as a ubiquitous one-tap control (Separate / Combine / Compare / Store Summary) on every report (RICS proves this is table-stakes for multi-store). Bar charts with hover % of total + sortable columnar table + export.
- Channel comparison: in-store POS vs online vs phone/B2B vs memo/consignment as a first-class sales dimension (currently unmodeled — add a channel field on Sales Invoice).
- Store-level Sales Target doctype (store × day/week/month × revenue/units/UPT target) distinct from employee Performance Target, driving a live pacing-to-target gauge + forecast/projected-day-close run-rate (Toast/Lightspeed pattern).
- Salesperson leaderboard ranked on REVENUE + UPT + conversion + attach rate + margin + customer-capture-rate — not revenue/txn only (Lightspeed's per-employee Customer Capture Rate is a jewelry-critical CRM KPI).
- Slow-mover vs fast-mover velocity analysis surfaced in a sales dashboard: days-since-last-sale, sell-through %, days-of-inventory-remaining, dustiness — as an in-SPA panel, not iframe-embedded (fast/slow moving reports exist but are orphaned).
- Price-Point Summary (sales distribution by price band) and 20/80 attribution (auto-detect the ~20% of customers = 80% of sales and the handful of vendors driving most revenue) as cheap high-impact insights (The Edge pattern).
- Saved + scheduled reports (daily/weekly/monthly) auto-emailed to stakeholders, extended to Slack/WhatsApp push for owners (Lightspeed + RICS 'automated reports delivered your way').
- Insight-to-action loop: from a slow-mover/stockout/margin insight, surface a one-click action (create PO, adjust price, transfer stock between stores, flag for AIMS markdown) without leaving the screen (Lightspeed PO-from-report pattern).
- Period-over-period delta arrows (▲/▼) on every metric tile, 'Measure' in-place dropdown to swap metric without re-running, 'Format results' inline pivot (Shopify/Lightspeed ergonomics).
- Tri-mode inventory valuation (Active Selling Price / Average weighted Cost / Current Cost) and ROI/Turns/Sell-Thru % as first-class sort keys (RICS).
- Grid (Col×Row) matrix analytics for jewelry attribute matrices (karat × style, gem-type × setting) with per-cell sell-through and % of total (RICS grid pattern).

## Best-in-Class — Profit Intelligence

- TRUE jewelry COGS decomposition persisted per-invoice: gold-at-sale (rate locked at point-of-sale: gold_rate_at_sale/source/timestamp so historical COGS never drifts) + gemstone + labor/bench + making charge + alloy + commission + payment-processing cost + overhead allocation. Zevar's sale_cost_breakdown already does this — harden it as the authoritative spine (Valigara/PIRO/Jewel360/Luxare depth; Lightspeed/The Edge/Shopify all lack it).
- Net/Contribution margin per SKU, not just gross: revenue − COGS − payment cost − commission − allocated overhead. Jewelers almost never see true contribution margin today (no competitor exposes it well — Zevar's moat).
- Margin Waterfall (Pricefx/Vendavo): visualize every step from list price → metal/gold surcharge → making charge → discount → associate discount → payment cost → overhead → NET pocket margin, color-coded green(adds)/red(leaks). Shows WHERE margin disappears.
- Price-Volume-Mix (PVM) / Margin Bridge analysis (Vendavo Margin Bridge Analyzer): decompose period-over-period gross-margin change into price effect, volume effect, mix/category effect, new/discontinued effect, and cost (gold-rate) effect — THE killer exec view; no jewelry POS has it.
- What-if / Market Simulation (Pricefx): interactive sliders for gold rate, making %, discount, overhead → live recompute of projected margin + elasticity-adjusted volume + revenue impact + confidence band, before applying. Store as a What-if Simulation Run record for replay/audit.
- Live spot metal pricing model: per-metal purity mapping (10k=41%, etc.) + live Kitco-equivalent feed + fallback chain + optional POS prompt-with-timeout + instant repricing of all affected items; gold-rate pass-through % metric and unrealized gain/loss on inventory (historical vs replacement cost) (The Edge Spot Metal + Valigara + Jewel360 synthesis).
- Floor-price / 'lowest acceptable price' shown alongside margin in inventory AND at POS as a negotiation guardrail; block-with-override or warn-with-reason on margin-floor breach; capture override reason (The Edge floor-price + Luxare rule-based minimum margin thresholds).
- Margin Heatmap by item/category/associate/store/season and category×associate, store×metal, color intensity = margin %, click-through to transactions (Zevar get_margin_heatmap exists — extend dimensions).
- Activity-Based Costing (ABC) overhead absorption: cost pools by store (rent/utilities/insurance/marketing/depreciation) + cost drivers (per-invoice, revenue-proportional, per-sqft/display-case, per-associate-hour, per-repair-bench-hour) as a 3rd method beyond Zevar's 2 (Netscape ABC + multi-store).
- AIMS-style aged-inventory margin-recovery engine: monthly batch that previews Old-vs-New price, applies tiered spiffs (bonus commission to move old stock) AND markdowns, auto-generates staff-facing spiff/bonus posters + customer-facing in-case clearance signage — category-unique, no generic POS has it (The Edge AIMS).
- Open-to-Buy (OTB) wizard: forward purchasing schedule from prior-3-year weighted COGS, splitting Total/Special-Order/Repeat/Net COGS, factoring on-hand + on-order — forward capital-allocation intelligence (The Edge OTB).
- AI margin recommendations grounded in RAG over the store's own historical events (confidence_level + reasoning + rag_sources), wired to live margin events, delivered as actionable cards + approval workflow (Zevar Pricing Recommendation already has the bones — extend to Pricefx-Agents-style continuous monitors).
- Markdown/slow-mover optimization algorithm: aging buckets + sales velocity + sell-through target → suggested clearance price that maximizes recovered margin (Increff/RELEX/Yieldigo — Zevar's Clearance enum needs real velocity math).
- Returns-with-$-GP-lost and discount-leakage analytics: separate line-item vs order-level vs shipping discounts, return fees, return reasons, returned-quantity-rate; discount $/count/avg-%/Ratio per associate (The Edge Selling-Skills + Shopify discount decomposition).
- Margin data-quality guardrail: flag items sold at cost / with zero/missing cost entered (Lightspeed Margin Alert pattern — very common jewelry data problem).
